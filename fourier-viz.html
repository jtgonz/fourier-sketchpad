the Fourier Explor-ier

This is an interactive visualization of the discrete Fourier transform.

Sketch a circle (or any other shape) in the window. You'll see that your 'analog' signal has been sampled at frequency N. see an example

  • 'n' to toggle the labels.
  • 'r' to toggle the resolution.
  • ENTER to continue.

Entering the next mode, you'll see that we've plotted a unit circle with N discrete points. Generally speaking, a point on the unit circle can be represented like this:

By incrementing the value of n, we can step through the N points of our circle.

  • RIGHT to increment n by one.
  • LEFT to decrement n by one.

If we'd like to move through the circle at a faster pace (a higher frequency), we can add a multiplier k to our expression. Now if we increment n by one, we move through n * k points on the unit circle.

  • UP to increment k by one.
  • DOWN to decrement k by one.

The red vector points to value of the signal at n. The blue vector points to the corresponding point on the unit circle (given both n and k). The gray vector is the product of both of these:
